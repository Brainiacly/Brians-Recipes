Brian's Crispy Marshmallow Candy

🥄 Ingredients and Supplies Instructions Quick Recipe Info Additional Information Other Sites 🥣

This homemade marshmallow candy is crispy, wafer-like, sweet, and fun to make. When finished, it becomes thin and brittle, similar to peanut brittle, but white and off-white with a light crispy texture.

🥄 Ingredients and Supplies 🍫

  • About 2 cups of Jet-Puffed Mini Marshmallows
  • Parchment paper
  • Optional Magic Shell topping
  • Kitchen supplies
    • A microwave-safe paper plate or regular plate
    • A spoon or spatula for pushing the edges back
    • A microwave
    • A freezer

🧑‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Cut a piece of parchment paper to fit the size of a paper plate or regular plate.
  2. Place the parchment paper on the plate.
  3. Spread about 2 cups of mini marshmallows across the surface of the parchment paper.
  4. Microwave the marshmallows for 30 seconds at a time.
  5. After each 30-second interval, carefully remove the plate from the microwave.
  6. The marshmallows will fluff up and may start to move toward the edges of the plate.
  7. Gently push the corners and edges back toward the center with a spoon or spatula.
  8. Wait briefly for the marshmallows to deflate before microwaving again.
  9. Repeat the process as needed. In most microwaves, this may take up to four 30-second intervals.
  10. Immediately remove the plate from the microwave when the marshmallow candy is ready.
  11. Place the plate in the freezer for 5 minutes.
  12. For an optional finish, add a small amount of Magic Shell topping halfway through the freezer time.
  13. Return the plate to the freezer for another 5 minutes.
  14. Remove the plate from the freezer and carefully peel the candy from the parchment paper.
  15. Break the candy into pieces and enjoy your crispy homemade treat.

💡 Quick Recipe Info

Total Time: About 17 minutes

Serving Size: Serves 1 to 4 people, depending on their appetite.

Estimated Calories: About 300 calories for the full recipe.

Estimated Sugar: About 51 grams of sugar for the full recipe.

Per Serving Estimate: About 75 to 300 calories and 13 to 51 grams of sugar, depending on serving size.
